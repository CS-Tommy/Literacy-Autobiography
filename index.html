My Literacy Autobiography

- Tommy Li

Authorial Intent

With the rise of technology, what better way to tell a story than through technology itself? This website tells the story of how my literacy has developed throughout different parts of my life. Each moments highlights a different part of my literacy journey, from taking ESL classes to my interest in technology, etc. Rather than presenting my story in a traditional essay, I chose a website format because it's closely related to the journey itself. Creating websites is also something I enjoy doing, partly because it's a user-friendly way to pass on information.

These moments ultimately represent different "voices" and influences that shaped how I communicate and understand the world. The layout follows a chronological order, arranged from having the least to most impact on my literacy today. Clicking the button within each moment would showcase how that moment has shaped my journey today and possibly even connect to a reading!

Inspired by ideas from scholars like Bakhtin and Freire, this project explores how literacy is not just reading and writing, but a process of taking in different perspectives, institutions, and communities.

Moments That Shaped My Literacy

ESL

Having to start school at first grade with no English experience forced me to be placed into ESL. I would sit in the back of the classroom with the teacher while the rest of the class is working on a different activity. During those times, I would consistently feel like I'm missing out on bonding with the other students. However, I still enjoyed my time taking ESL. The teacher was super patient and it helped create a stronger bond between the teacher and I.

Split Systems

Although I was born in the U.S, I moved to China when I was a baby and came back to the States when I was around 7. Learning English was hard because I had already learnt the basic rules for Chinese so transitioning to a whole different language system wasn't the easiest. My English didn't start significantly improving until middle school and even throughout high school, I still had trouble with grammar. I'm glad I've adapted to the English system now, but also disappointed that I lost knowledge of another system in the process.

Fitness

Towards the end of high school, I met a guy that was always in my classes but I never talked to. One day during the summer, he asked me if I wanted to goto the gym with him. I said yes because I had nothing else to do. From that day till now, I've been working out for the past 3-4 years (with some questionable long breaks). I've immersed myself into gym culture and made it part of my daily routine to do something active. If I haven't met that guy (who I still talk to to this day), I probably wouldn't be who I am today.

Robotics

In middle school, one of my friends told me to sign up for this robotics class because apparently all they did was have free time on the chromebooks. We both later found out that was not the case and my friend ended up dropping the class. That was my first time working with code. I was always the coder for a three man team and that sort of made me grow to like coding. The majors I've picked in college and internships I've applied for all stemmed from that one robotics class. I've also somewhat learned coding lingo and what it was really like to be someone staring at code for hours.

Different

My mom forced me to enroll in a middle school that was close to our neighborhood. Because of that, I was in a school where I was basically the only Asian kid. Due to the need of fitting in, I of course tried my best to copy how the students interacted, their lingo, and just their overall demeanor.
